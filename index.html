Health in the Margins

Health in the Margins is a grassroots community organisation working to reimagine health, care, and healing by centring subjugated knowledge, lived experience, and community wisdom.

What We Do

We create spaces for reflection, dialogue, and action that challenge dominant approaches to health. Our work centres those most affected by health inequities and supports communities to reclaim and define their own health practices.

About Us

Julianna

Julianna is the founder of Health in the Margins and works at the intersection of research, lived experience, and epistemic justice. Trained as a medical anthropologist and epidemiologist, her work examines how dominant approaches to health shape whose knowledge is valued and whose is excluded.

Through Health in the Margins, Julianna is committed to creating spaces where subjugated health knowledge is re-centred and communities most affected by health inequities are supported to lead their own practices of care and healing.

Katheryn Lotsos

Katheryn Lotsos is a psychotherapist whose work sits at the intersection of mental health and social justice. For over 25 years, she has worked alongside marginalised communities globally to co-create healing spaces grounded in lived experience and collective care.

As a Director at Health in the Margins, Katheryn brings a strong commitment to trauma-informed, decolonial, and anti-oppressive approaches, and believes health justice requires confronting power within systems of care.
