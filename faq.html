Frequently Asked Questions

Epistemic justice means honouring and restoring the knowledge, traditions, and lived experiences of frontline communities. We believe everyone’s ways of knowing should shape how health is defined, researched, and delivered.

We co-create every project with those most impacted by health injustice. Community voices, leadership, and priorities guide our work from start to finish.

Frontline communities are those most affected by health injustice and whose knowledge and experiences are often overlooked.

We challenge dominant global health models by centring community knowledge and leadership. Our work is participatory, inclusive, and rooted in justice, aiming for real, lasting change in health equity.
